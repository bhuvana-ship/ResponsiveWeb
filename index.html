Sye Raa Narasimha Reddy

The story is set around 10 years before India's First War of Independence. Uyyalawada Narasimha Reddy (Chiranjeevi) is depicted as the emperor of the Rayalaseema region of Andhra Pradesh.[16] Lakshmi (Tamannaah) is Temple dancer who is also a love interest of Narasimha. After Narasimha expresses his love and promises to marry Lakshmi, Reddy comes to know that he is already married to Siddhamma (Nayanthara) when he was a kid due to a ritual that helps rains in the region. Elders convince Reddy to perform the same ritual again with Siddhamma, which he agrees for the betterment of region. Knowing this Lakshmi tries to suicide but Reddy convinces her to live and asks her to use her dance form to influence people. Laksmi understands and leaves the Renadu region. Siddhamma expresses her love towards Reddy which she has been hiding from childhood. Reddy then living happily his mother (Lakshmi Gopalaswamy), wife and other family members. During the same time, the British East India Company comes to India for business but soon starts to loot, torture the natives, carry the natural wealth of India back to Britain. When Narasimha Reddy witnesses their atrocities, he gets furious and leaves his throne, wife, mother and teams up with palegars (leader of other villages), Veera Reddy (Jagapathi Babu), Avuku Raju (Sudeep), and starts rebellion against them. Raja Pandi (Vijay Sethupathi) a leader from Madras (now Chennai) joins him. They all start a rebellion against the British East India Company. The rest of the movie is about how Reddy with all his supporters fight against the British rule.

About

Historians across India mentioned that the rebellion of Uyyalawada Narasimha Reddy is not about Nationalism as shown in the movie. He only fought with British for not getting his monthly pension. Nekkanti Srinivasa Rao, a Telugu historian, told to media that the movie lacks historical accuracy in the story of Uyyalawada Narasimha Reddy by Paruchuri brothers. Srinivasa Rao said that Narasimha Reddy only questioned the British authorities for his monthly pension which he was supposed to inherit from his grandfather.
